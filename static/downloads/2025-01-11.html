QuizSage Bug at Meet 2 Fixed

I apologize for a QuizSage bug that resulted in some confusion and erroneous team-to-quiz assignments at Meet 2. The bug was entirely my fault. Combined with some late registration data changes, some I heard of only the morning of the meet, QuizSage data got messed up in some places in ugly ways. After the meet, I found and fixed the bug that caused all this, and I also cleaned up the Meet 2 data so that the statistics and quiz review all show what actually happened at the meet.

Here’s what happened: Meet 2 had 50% more teams and 33% fewer Preliminary quizzes relative to the past meets in which we’ve used QuizSage. What this means practically is that the probability of team point ties in the Preliminary bracket were significantly higher, and thus we ended up with several ties. For the meet state page (which lists out the schedule sets), QuizSage sorts teams by points and assigns them locations. So 27th place by Preliminary points might be on the left in Auxiliary quiz 1, and 26st place might be center in Auxiliary quiz 5. But in the case where 27th place and 26th place are tied, QuizSage had no instructions on how to sort them. So like HAL9000, it started acting strange, randomly flipping tied teams whenever the state page reloaded. Bad, bad, bad.

To fix this, I told QuizSage to sort teams based on a deeper set of criteria than simply their team points:

  1. Points average for Preliminary quizzes (which is the limit of what QuizSage was doing before)
  2. Points sum for the Preliminary quizzes (which probably won’t make any difference, but QS will try this step anyway)
  3. Highest cumulative positions in Preliminary quizzes (i.e. 1st + 3nd > 3rd + 2nd )
  4. Total bonus team points in Preliminary quizzes
  5. Greater total number of quizzes > fewer total number of quizzes (which for Prelims won’t make any difference)
  6. Fewer total number of quizzers > more total number of quizzers

In theory, I would be shocked if any ties didn’t get sorted by the end of step 4. However, in likely very rare cases where a tie still exists, I designed steps 5 and 6 to favor what would seem like the stronger of the 2 teams.

Part of the fallout of this bug was it resulted in 2 teams not getting an Auxiliary quiz and 2 other teams getting 2 Auxiliary quizzes (instead of the 1 quiz each team was suppose to get). This results in teams in Auxiliary not having a fair set of quizzes upon which to be evaluated for statistics purposes. So after much consideration, Andrew and I decided to weight the Auxiliary bracket at 0 for Meet 2. (Normally, it’s set at 50% this season, at least until we have a few meets of good full-meet data so we can consider adjustments based on that data.)

Encourage Electronic Use of QuizSage

I’ve noticed a few folks printing out the meet state report and the type distribution page from QuizSage for use during the meet. You’re of course free to do this if you want, but I recommend against it. Instead, I recommend using a smartphone and/or tablet. The reason being: Unfortunately, roster data can and does change after the registration deadline. And depending on the changes, it can become unavoidable for a whole draw to need to be rebuilt. So hardcopy schedules, distributions, and rosters can easily become deprecated. And if that happens after announcements, we don’t have an easy way to communicate that to every participant and spectator at the meet.

Moreover, by using a smartphone and/or tablet, participants and spectators can get up-to-date statistics and meet state reports. And they can also view live scoreboards for each room.

Coaching Notes

I have some specific coaching notes to go over with you; but before that, I’d like to share with you an overall governing philosophy about coaching:

Coaches are unavoidably spiritual and practical mentors who need to exemplify the behaviors we want to encourage in our youth quizzers. We ask our youth quizzers to establish and maintain a routine memorization schedule, setting aside dedicated time every week to deeply focus on verses of Scripture, writing them on their hearts with as much attention to detail as possible, ideally verbatim with reference. It would be hypocritical of us adults to not devote an equivalent level of focus to our coaching responsibilities. As we hold youth quizzers accountable to high standards of professionalism, so should we hold ourselves accountable.

As coaches, we need to be reliable, proactive, trustworthy, encouraging, and practically helpful.

OK, now on to the specific coaching notes.

Meet Registration Process

To review, the meet registration process is as follows:

  1. All coaches have access via the PNW Quizzing web site to the season schedule and meet registration forms, which become editable immediately after the preceding meet.
  2. Coaches can edit and update their registration as often as they’d like after the preceding meet and before the registration deadline of the next meet.
  3. The registration deadline for the next meet is 2 weeks prior to the next meet, at which point the registration form locks until after the meet.
  4. Registration data can only be changed after the deadline by contacting the Coordinator.

Registration data is used to build the draw, schedule, and roster. It’s also used by the host organization for housing and lunch planning purposes. It’s the expectation that all coaches will hold themselves accountable to ensure their registration data is complete and accurate prior to each deadline. If there are any questions, difficulties coaches encounter, or anticipated situations where a coach won’t be able to ensure complete and accurate registration data is in the system before the deadline, coaches should raise these to the Coordinator before the registration deadline.

There may occur situations beyond a coach’s control where changes become necessary after the deadline, such as a quizzer becoming sick or a family emergency becoming the priority. Then, it’s the expectation the coach will contact the Coordinator as soon as possible about these data changes. A coach can and should solicit from quizzers their intention to participate in the upcoming meet prior to the registration deadline; therefore, the only cases where a coach can’t foresee and forestall post-deadline data changes are for unanticipatory health, weather, or home/family crises.

(While there is an automated reminder email sent a week before each registration deadline, and occasionally a manual email from the Coordinator sent prior to the deadline, it is solely the responsibility of the coach to ensure his or her registration data is complete and accurate prior to each deadline. As a historical aside, I harken back to the mid-1990s. In that era, to register for a meet, you had to photocopy a registration form, fill it out by hand, then fax it to the Pacific Northwest church district office. This all had to be done 2 weeks prior to the meet. There were no reminders.)

To help encourage accountability to these expectations, I am considering asking coaches to publicly share their avoidable post-deadline registration changes with the assembly during meet announcements.

Hosting is a Burden; Let’s Make it Light

Bible Quizzing can realistically only operate when there are team organizations willing to host meets. Hosts provide a facility to house meet operations, they handle room and event space setup, and they coordinate with the building’s owners and build equipment’s operators. Hosts may optionally offer overnight housing for away teams, and hosts optionally offer lunch on site. As PNW Quizzing participation grows, the necessity for overnight housing grows, and thus the burden for hosts increases.

To ensure sustainability of Quizzing operations over the long-term, we all need to do whatever we can to keep the burden on hosts as light as possible. To that end, please:

  • Ensure your registration data is complete and accurate prior to the deadline
  • When possible, avoid requiring housing and lunch stipulations
    • Certainly include notes about allergies, though
  • Remind your quizzers that host-provided overnight housing is not a hotel
    • They should bring a sleeping bag, a towel, and optionally a pillow
    • They should be aware that a clean floor is a housing option; a bed is a luxury

Open Book Opportunities

After each meet, I invest some time analyzing the statistics, and I noticed something from Meet 2: Several quizzers from Meet 2 ended up getting 0s for the meet when they could have picked up points from open book opportunities. Picking up a point, even just 1 point for the meet, is better than nothing and can help spark additional motivation to study more between meets. And open book is often a gateway to that 1 point.

One of the easiest ways to pick up an open book point is through use of a Finish query list, which is nothing more than an alphabetically sorted list of all verses for the meet. This is especially true for “foreign” translations. For example, if you’ve memorized NIV and you’re in a quiz with all-NIV quizzers, picking up a Finish from the ESV via open book is relatively easy. You can usually trigger on the third or fourth word reliably.

An easy way to generate this sort of Finish list is by using QuizSage’s Reference Generator. Login to QuizSage, and then from the home page, click the “Reference Generator” link. Ensure the “Material Label/Description” covers the full material for the meet (all translations), and ensure the “Alphabetical Material from First Words” checkbox is checked. Then print the resulting generated document.

A team can share a single printed reference material. In fact, you can share a single reference across teams if you want.

Using QuizSage’s Memorization Tools

Another note about preparation: Quizzers who use QuizSage’s memorization tools score better at quiz meets than quizzers who don’t. The 3 main tools are:

  • Initial Memorization — for tracking verses memorized
  • Memorization Review — for reviewing verses memorized
  • Memory State — for reporting on verses memorized and to share memory state with others

Quizzers can share their memory states with their coaches, which provides the coach the ability to:

  • Help keep their quizzers accountable for routine memorization
  • Build pick-up quizzes based on material the quizzers at practice have memorized

Coaches Can Appeal

Remember, a coach can appeal a ruling just like a quizzer; however, with great power comes great responsibility. The purpose of appeals is not to be right nor become right but to determine what is right. The appeal process exists to improve rulings so they’re accurate, objective, consistent, clear, and thereby fair. Ergo, appeals can be preceded by questions, requests for information, etc. without invalidating your ability to appeal.

As a coach, if you’re unsure about something, please ask the QM. And if you notice something that you think is contrary to the rule book, please raise that with the QM.

Some useful documents for coaches to have bookmarked on your smartphone or tablet then are:

  • CBQ Rule Book (https://cbqz.org/CBQ_system/rule_book.md)
  • Magistrate Best Practices (https://cbqz.org/tenets/magistrate_best_practices.md)

Governance Updates

As a reminder, there are 2 governance organizations to consider for Quizzing. “PNW Quizzing” is the self-governing, self-instantiating regional organization currently covering the geography of Washington, Oregon, and Northern Idaho. “Christian Bible Quizzing” or CBQ is the international organization that “oversees and supports local and regional Quizzing operations internationally with the mission to encourage the most people to memorize the most verses of Scripture.”

As PNW Quizzing grows, there will come a time (perhaps fast approaching) when it’ll be practically valuable (for optimized missional outcomes) to split our single region up into separate regions, collaborating and occasionally even quizzing together, but typically running separate local season schedules. Even now, our vast geography may be a limiting factor to maximal outcomes. It would take nearly 20 hours to travel round trip from Post Falls, Idaho to Medford, Oregon.
